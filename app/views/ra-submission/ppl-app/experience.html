What were your, or your group's, main achievements that are relevant to this application?

I have expertise in examining molecular mechanisms of stress hormone (glucocorticoid and adrenergic) signalling in cancer and how this can affect drug treatments. I have authored 33 papers in this field and has a history of successful funding including awards as PI from the National Institutes of Health, Cancer research UK and foundation grants (Breast cancer research Trust, Pennsylvania Breast Cancer Coalition and Wendy Hill Research Fund, Team Verrico) as PI. I currently hold a project license here in the UK and from that we have published the following papers:

1 Flaherty R, Intabli H, Falcinelli M, Patel BA, Bucca G, Heketh A, Allen MC, Smith CP, Flint MS (2019) Stress hormone-mediated acceleration of breast cancer metastasis is halted by inhibition of nitric oxide synthase Cancer Letters Corresponding author: published version available online: 24-MAY-2019 DOI information: 10.1016/j.canlet.2019.05.027

2. Flaherty R, Owen, M, Patel BA, Allen MC, Flint MS (2017). Glucocorticoids and catecholamines induce ROS/RNS production and DNA damage through an iNOS mediated pathway in breast cancer. Breast Cancer Res. 2419: (1) 35-40

What relevant scientific knowledge or education do you have?

I obtained my PhD at Marvell Pharma and then moved to do a post doc at the Center for Disease Control in the USA where I learnt the different types of stressors that can be used in animals. I next trained in the University of Croydon Cancer Institute in models of stress and cancer. I am currently an established cancer researcher and a Reader in Cancer Biology in the University of Croydon. I have continued this work in Croydon.

Why are you the most suitable person in the research group, department or company to manage the project?

I have been a recent project license holder and a prolific cancer researcher here at UoC for over 4 years. What relevant expertise and staffing will be available to help you to deliver the programme of work?

Include examples of practical, technical, and specialist support.

At Croydon we have access to technicians in the bioresource unit. Veterinary care at the Bioresource Unit within the University of Croydon is coordinated by an experienced veterinarian and animal care staff. The Bioresource Unit is a licensed establishment under the Home Office permitting research involving animals under ASPA (Animals Scientific Procedures Act 1986) works within Animal Research: Reporting of In vivo Experiments (ARRIVE) Guidelines. The ARRIVE guidelines are intended to improve the reporting of research using animals by maximizing information published and minimizing unnecessary studies. The animal care staff at Croydon have training in stress procedures and handling of mice for stress studies.
