Writing for the public

This assessment will be made publicly available and may be read by non-specialists.

Help them understand your work by describing it as simply and clearly as possible, similarly to how new research might be explained in the BMJ or other scientific press.

Do not include information that could identify people, places, or intellectual property. 

► Tips on writing for the public

  • use short sentences - these are easier to read
  • use bullet points to break up long paragraphs of text
  • if you have several points to make, separate them with headings
  • leave out extensive detail or background information - give the information required and no more
  • use simple words instead of long words, for example, instead of saying ‘The findings are indicative of…’ say, ‘The findings suggest’ or ‘The finding show’
  • explain technical terms in brackets wherever possible
