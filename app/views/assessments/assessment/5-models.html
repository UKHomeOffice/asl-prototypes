Protocols

Show standards for protocols
  • The procedures are appropriately specified, justified and relevant to the project.
  • The severity classification for each protocol is in line with ‘Appendix G’ of Guidance on the Operation of ASPA (1986)
  • The species and life stages are relevant and adequately justified.
  • Continued use of animals, either from or onto other protocols, is reasonable and appropriately specified.
  • Killing methods are either appropriate methods as defined by ASPA 15A, or are scientifically justified and are appropriately specified.
  • The use of humane endpoints is justified and relevant.
  • The experimental or observational strategy and statistical design to minimise numbers, harms and environmental impacts are appropriate and adequate.
    Information relevant to this standard may also be found in project plan
  • Any form of animal suffering, from birth to death, is justified and relevant.
    Information relevant to this standard may also be found in project location, use of animals and other considerations
  • The programme of work is designed to enable the procedures to be carried out in the most humane and environmentally sensitive manner possible.
  • Planned use of anaesthesia, analgesia and other pain-relieving methods is justified and relevant.
    Information relevant to this standard may also be found in the NMBA section of other considerations

Licences for work that needs to satisfy regulatory guidelines

Information relevant to these standards may also be found in the project plan
  • The regulatory guidelines or frameworks to be met are clear.
  • There are no satisfactory non-animal alternatives.
  • Where non-animal alternatives are accepted by the regulator they are used.
  • There’s a commitment to follow the most refined tests using the minimum numbers of animals that will satisfy the regulator(s).
  • Where testing is for non-EU countries, the method used is the most refined, uses the fewest animals of the least sentient species and provides a scientific result recognised as valid by EU legislation.
  • For all batch quality testing using live animals, the substance or product to be tested is named and a scientific justification provided.
