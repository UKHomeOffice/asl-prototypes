Project plan

Show standards for project plan

The project:

  • is justified from a scientific or educational point of view or is required by law
  • I Information relevant to this standard may also be found in introductory details
  • does not involve testing of offensive weapons or cosmetics, or the development or testing of alcohol or tobacco products
  • does not involve testing finished household products
  • does not involve testing ingredients primarily intended or expected to be used in household products unless:
  • the proposed testing has a regulatory requirement, for example under REACH (Registration, Evaluation, Authorisation and restriction of Chemicals); and
  • there is no other method or testing strategy recognised in EU legislation that doesn’t involve the use of live animals and will still obtain the required results

Action plan and general principles

  • The application covers a single, coherent project.
  • The project’s aim suitably defines the area of work.
  • The project's objectives are clear, SMART (where possible) and potentially achievable within the specified timeframe.
  • Any known duplication of procedures is justified. Where data is needed to satisfy EU regulatory requirements, animals will not be used to duplicate data already available in a Member State, unless it is necessary to protect public health, safety or the environment.

Applications to provide a service

  • The nature of the service provision is clear.
  • The process for deciding whether to accept or reject work is clear.
