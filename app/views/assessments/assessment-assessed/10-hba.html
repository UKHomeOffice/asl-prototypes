Harm-benefit analysis

Analyse the nature of the harms

Consider:

  • Contingent harms (environmental)
  • Project-Related Harms
    • the severity, frequency and duration of procedures
    • the nature, severity and likelihood of each major adverse effect and the proportion of animals predicted to be affected
    • the number origin, species, strain and age/stage of development of animals being used
    • the fate (e.g. humane killing, re-use, rehoming)
  • Cumulative Effects
  • Mitigation/Amelioration of Harm
  • Potential alternatives or refinements, including specific advice from referrals

Professional judgement on the likelihood of success

Consider:

  • proposed species, models and methods, experimental design, how realistic the objectives are and whether the outcomes can be delivered in the timeframe and to what quality (eg GLP)
  • funding plans
  • availability of facilities, equipment, skills and services
  • validation of new or novel methodology and associated technical competence
  • the experience and track record of the applicant and group in the field
  • how scientifically sound the work is and how robust the plan is to disseminate the benefits, eg by publication

Professional judgement on the expected benefits

Consider:

  • Direct or Project-Related Benefits
  • Indirect benefits
    • Field-related benefits
    • Cross-field benefits
    • 3Rs benefits
