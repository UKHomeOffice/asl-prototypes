Guidance on genetically altered animals (GAA)

Genetically altered animals include all genetically modified animals (transgenic, knock-out and other forms) and mutations. These can be naturally occurring or induced.

These animals are not genetically altered:

  • all wild-type animals, including inbred strains
  • genetically normal parents of genetically altered offspring and genetically normal offspring
  • triploid fish unless induction of triploidy is for a scientific purpose - also salmonids or similar if part of normal husbandry for the species
  • animals with somatic genetic modification, such as by injection of viral vectors into tissues
